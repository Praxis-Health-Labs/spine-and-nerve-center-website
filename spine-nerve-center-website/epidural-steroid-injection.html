TREATMENT OVERVIEW

Epidural Steroid Injection

An epidural steroid injection is a common medical procedure used to manage pain and inflammation associated with various conditions, particularly spinal or nerve-related issues. During the procedure, a combination of corticosteroid medication and a local anesthetic is injected into the epidural space around the spinal cord.

PROCEDURE DETAILS

What is the Procedure?

During an epidural steroid injection, a healthcare provider administers a combination of corticosteroid medication and a local anesthetic into the epidural space near the spinal cord and affected nerve roots.

This procedure is commonly used to alleviate pain and inflammation associated with conditions like herniated discs, spinal stenosis, or radiculopathy. The corticosteroid medication reduces inflammation and suppresses the immune response, while the local anesthetic provides immediate pain relief.

The injection is typically guided by X-ray or fluoroscopy to ensure precise placement. While the effects can vary, many patients experience pain relief that can last for several weeks to months, allowing for improved mobility and participation in physical therapy or other pain management strategies.

Quick Facts

  • • Procedure Duration: 15-30 minutes
  • • Recovery Time: 30-60 minutes observation
  • • Pain Relief Onset: Within 2-7 days
  • • Success Rate: 70-90% experience improvement

TREATMENT ADVANTAGES

What are the Benefits?

Epidural steroid injections offer several benefits, providing targeted relief from pain and inflammation in the spinal region, making them a valuable tool in managing various conditions.

Targeted Pain Relief

Delivers medication directly to the source of inflammation, providing focused relief exactly where it's needed most.

Minimally Invasive

Outpatient procedure that can contribute to improved quality of life for individuals suffering from debilitating pain.

Improved Mobility

Enables patients to engage in physical therapy or other rehabilitative measures more effectively by reducing pain.

Quick Procedure

Typically completed within 15-30 minutes with minimal downtime, allowing for same-day return to activities.

Long-lasting Relief

Effects can last several weeks to months, providing extended pain relief and reduced inflammation.

Surgery Alternative

May help patients avoid or delay the need for more invasive surgical procedures.

IMPORTANT INFORMATION

What are the Risks and Side Effects?

Epidural steroid injections are generally safe, but there are potential risks and side effects to be aware of. These may include:

Common Side Effects

  • Temporary increase in pain
  • Bleeding or infection at injection site
  • Nerve damage (rare)
  • Facial flushing or insomnia

Rare Complications

  • Allergic reactions
  • Changes in blood sugar levels
  • Temporary changes in blood pressure
  • Headaches

Important: The risk of complications is minimized through precise injection techniques and proper medical supervision. It's essential for patients to discuss their medical history, allergies, and any concerns with their healthcare provider before the procedure. Post-injection care, including monitoring for any unusual symptoms, is crucial to ensuring a safe and effective experience.

PREPARATION GUIDE

How to Prepare for the Procedure?

Preparing for an epidural steroid injection involves a few key steps. Patients should communicate any relevant medical history, allergies, or current medications with their healthcare provider during pre-procedure consultations.

1

Medical Review

Discuss your medical history, current medications, and any allergies with your healthcare provider.

2

Medication Adjustments

May need to temporarily stop blood thinners or adjust medications as directed by your doctor.

3

Day of Procedure

Wear comfortable clothing and avoid jewelry. Follow fasting instructions if sedation will be used.

4

Transportation

Arrange for someone to drive you home after the procedure, especially if sedation is used.

Pre-Procedure Checklist

  • Medical history reviewed
  • Medications adjusted as needed
  • Transportation arranged
  • Questions prepared for doctor
  • Insurance verified

RECOVERY GUIDE

What is the Post-Procedure Care?

After an epidural steroid injection, patients should plan for some post-procedure care. It's advisable to rest for the remainder of the day and refrain from strenuous activities.

First 24 Hours

Rest and avoid strenuous activities. Apply ice to the injection site to reduce discomfort. Monitor for any unusual symptoms.

Days 2-3

Gradually return to normal activities as tolerated. Continue taking prescribed pain medications as directed. Stay well-hydrated.

First Week

Most patients can resume regular activities. Begin physical therapy if recommended. Monitor pain levels and report improvements to your provider.

Follow-up Visit

Attend scheduled follow-up appointment to evaluate effectiveness and discuss next steps in your treatment plan.

When to Contact Your Doctor

  • Severe headache that doesn't improve
  • Fever over 101°F
  • Signs of infection at injection site
  • New or worsening neurological symptoms
