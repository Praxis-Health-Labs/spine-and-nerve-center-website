COMPREHENSIVE APPROACH

What is Medication Management?

Medication management is a systematic approach to optimizing drug therapy for chronic pain conditions. It involves careful selection, dosing, monitoring, and adjustment of medications to achieve the best possible outcomes while minimizing risks and side effects.

Our comprehensive program begins with a thorough evaluation of your medical history, current medications, pain patterns, and treatment goals. Dr. Fura develops a personalized medication plan that may include various classes of pain medications, each selected for its specific benefits in treating your condition.

This is not a one-size-fits-all approach. We regularly monitor your response to medications, adjusting doses and combinations as needed to maintain effectiveness while preventing tolerance, dependence, or adverse effects. Our goal is to use the minimum effective dose to achieve maximum pain relief and functional improvement.

IMPORTANT INFORMATION

What are the Risks and Considerations?

While medication management is generally safe when properly supervised, it's important to understand potential risks and side effects. We carefully monitor for these and adjust treatment as needed.

Common Side Effects

  • Drowsiness or fatigue, especially when starting new medications
  • Gastrointestinal issues such as nausea or constipation
  • Dry mouth or changes in appetite
  • Mild dizziness or lightheadedness

Important Considerations

  • Risk of dependency with certain medications
  • Potential drug interactions with other medications
  • Need for regular monitoring of liver and kidney function
  • Importance of adhering to prescribed dosages

Important: We use a multimodal approach to minimize risks, combining different types of medications at lower doses to achieve better results with fewer side effects. Regular monitoring and open communication help ensure safe and effective treatment.

MEDICATION TYPES

Classes of Medications We May Use

We utilize various medication classes, often in combination, to achieve optimal pain control with minimal side effects.

Anti-Inflammatory Medications

NSAIDs and other anti-inflammatory drugs to reduce inflammation and associated pain.

Neuropathic Pain Medications

Specialized medications for nerve pain, including anticonvulsants and certain antidepressants.

Muscle Relaxants

Medications to reduce muscle spasms and tension that contribute to pain.

Analgesics

Various pain relievers, carefully selected and monitored for safety and effectiveness.

Topical Medications

Creams, gels, and patches applied directly to painful areas for localized relief.

Adjuvant Medications

Supporting medications that enhance pain relief or manage medication side effects.

Patient Responsibilities

  • Take medications exactly as prescribed
  • Report any side effects promptly
  • Attend all scheduled follow-up appointments
  • Keep a pain and symptom diary
  • Inform us of all other medications and supplements
  • Never share medications with others
