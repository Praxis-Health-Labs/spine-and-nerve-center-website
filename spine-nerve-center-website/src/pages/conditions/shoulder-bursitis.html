CONDITION OVERVIEW

Shoulder Bursitis Treatment

Shoulder bursitis is a common cause of shoulder pain that occurs when the subacromial bursa—a small, fluid-filled sac that cushions the rotator cuff tendons from the bone above—becomes inflamed. This inflammation reduces the space within the shoulder joint, leading to a painful condition known as impingement. The pain is typically felt when lifting the arm to the side or overhead. While it can result from a direct injury, shoulder bursitis is most often an overuse condition that develops over time from repetitive activities.

ROOT CAUSES

What Leads to Shoulder Pain?

Shoulder bursitis is a key component of shoulder impingement syndrome. It is primarily caused by factors that irritate or crowd the bursa and rotator cuff tendons in the narrow space beneath the acromion bone.

Repetitive Overhead Motion

The most common cause. Sports like swimming and tennis, or occupations like painting and construction, repeatedly stress the shoulder, leading to inflammation.

Rotator Cuff Injury

Inflammation or tears in the underlying rotator cuff tendons can cause the overlying bursa to become secondarily inflamed and painful.

Poor Posture

Slouching or rounding the shoulders forward can narrow the space for the bursa and tendons, leading to chronic pinching and irritation with movement.

Direct Trauma

A direct fall or impact onto the shoulder can cause acute traumatic bursitis, leading to immediate pain and inflammation.

RECOGNIZING THE SIGNS

Common Symptoms

Shoulder bursitis symptoms often begin as a mild ache and can progress if the aggravating activity continues. The pain is typically linked to specific arm movements.

Pain on Outer Shoulder

A dull, aching pain localized to the top and outer area of the shoulder, which can sometimes radiate down the side of the arm.

Pain with Overhead Activity

Sharp pain when lifting the arm, especially when reaching for items on a high shelf, combing your hair, or throwing a ball.

Night Pain

Pain that worsens at night, making it difficult to fall asleep or causing you to wake up, especially when you roll onto the affected shoulder.

Painful Arc of Motion

A classic symptom where pain is most intense as the arm passes through a specific range (typically 60 to 120 degrees) when lifted out to the side.

Weakness and Stiffness

The affected shoulder may feel weak or stiff. This weakness is often due to pain inhibiting muscle function rather than actual muscle damage.

Tenderness to Touch

The point of the shoulder, specifically the front and side, may be tender when pressure is applied.
