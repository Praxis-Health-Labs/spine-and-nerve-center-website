TREATMENT OVERVIEW

Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation (TENS)

TENS therapy is a non-invasive, drug-free pain relief method that uses low-voltage electrical currents to interrupt pain signals. This portable, patient-controlled therapy provides on-demand relief for various chronic pain conditions, allowing you to manage pain actively throughout your daily activities without medication side effects or invasive procedures.

Quick Facts

  • • Treatment Duration: 20-60 minutes per session
  • • Frequency: Multiple times daily as needed
  • • Pain Relief Onset: Often immediate
  • • Success Rate: 60-80% report significant improvement

HOW IT WORKS

What is TENS Therapy?

TENS therapy works through two primary mechanisms. First, according to the gate control theory, electrical stimulation activates non-painful nerve fibers that "close the gate" to pain signals traveling to the brain. Second, the electrical stimulation triggers the release of endorphins, your body's natural pain-relieving chemicals.

The TENS unit is a small, battery-operated device connected to electrodes placed on your skin near the painful area. You control the intensity, frequency, and pattern of electrical pulses to find the most effective settings for your specific pain. The sensation is typically described as a pleasant tingling or buzzing feeling.

This therapy is completely non-invasive and drug-free, making it an excellent option for those seeking to reduce medication dependence or who cannot tolerate certain pain medications. Many patients use TENS as part of a comprehensive pain management strategy.

IMPORTANT INFORMATION

Precautions and Contraindications

While TENS therapy is generally very safe, there are important precautions and situations where it should not be used.

Do NOT Use If You Have

  • A cardiac pacemaker or defibrillator
  • Metal implants in the treatment area
  • Epilepsy or seizure disorders
  • Active bleeding or blood clotting disorders
  • Pregnancy (without doctor approval)

Avoid Placing Electrodes

  • On the head, face, or throat
  • Over the heart or chest
  • On irritated, infected, or broken skin
  • Over areas of reduced sensation
  • Near the eyes or genitals

USAGE GUIDELINES

Tips for Effective TENS Therapy

Following these guidelines will help you get the most benefit from your TENS therapy.

Best Practices

  • Clean skin thoroughly before applying electrodes
  • Replace electrodes when they lose stickiness
  • Start with low intensity and gradually increase
  • Vary electrode placement slightly to prevent skin irritation
  • Keep a pain diary to track effectiveness
  • Use during activities that typically trigger pain

Settings & Programs

  • Conventional: High frequency (50-100 Hz) for immediate relief
  • Acupuncture-like: Low frequency (2-4 Hz) for endorphin release
  • Burst: Combines both for comprehensive relief
  • Modulation: Varies settings to prevent adaptation
  • Adjust pulse width for comfort (typically 50-250 μs)
  • Intensity should be strong but comfortable

Maximizing Results

  • Use consistently for best results
  • Combine with gentle stretching
  • Stay hydrated during treatment
  • Don't use while driving or operating machinery
  • Remove before showering or swimming
  • Store electrodes properly to maintain adhesion

Who Can Benefit from TENS Therapy?

TENS therapy is effective for a wide range of acute and chronic pain conditions.

Back & Neck Pain

Chronic low back pain, cervical pain, muscle spasms, and postural strain.

Arthritis

Osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and other inflammatory joint conditions.

Neuropathic Pain

Diabetic neuropathy, post-herpetic neuralgia, and peripheral neuropathy.

Sports Injuries

Strains, sprains, tendinitis, and overuse injuries.

Post-Surgical Pain

Recovery pain following various surgical procedures.

Fibromyalgia

Widespread musculoskeletal pain and tender points.
