Critical Florida Deadlines You Must Know

Time is critical in Florida personal injury cases. There are two key deadlines you cannot miss:

  • 14-Day Rule for PIP: To use your auto insurance's PIP benefits, you must seek initial medical treatment within 14 days of your accident.
  • 2-Year Statute of Limitations: Florida law now requires most negligence lawsuits to be filed within two years of the accident. Waiting too long can mean losing your right to seek compensation entirely.

If you're missing documents, don't delay your appointment. We can help you obtain necessary records and ensure you meet these critical deadlines.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is acting fast so important after an accident in Florida?

Florida has strict deadlines. You must see a medical provider within 14 days to use your PIP auto insurance benefits. More importantly, the time limit to file a personal injury lawsuit has been shortened to just two years. Delay can jeopardize both your health and your legal rights.

How do recent law changes affect my injury case?

Florida now has a "modified comparative negligence" law. This means if you are found to be more than 50% at fault for the accident, you cannot recover any damages. This makes prompt medical evaluation and detailed documentation from a specialist like Dr. Fura more important than ever to clearly establish the extent of your injuries caused by the accident.

What if I don't have health insurance to cover treatment?

After your PIP benefits are used, we can work with your attorney on a Letter of Protection (LOP). An LOP is an agreement that allows you to receive necessary medical care without upfront costs. Our fees are then paid directly from your case settlement, ensuring you get the treatment you need to recover.
