No, you don't need a referral to schedule an appointment with Dr. Fura. However, some insurance plans may require a referral for coverage. We recommend checking with your insurance provider to understand your specific requirements.

We accept most major insurance plans including Medicare, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Aetna, Cigna, United Healthcare, and many others. Please contact our office at (813) 741-1071 to verify your specific insurance coverage.

Your initial consultation typically takes 45-60 minutes. This allows Dr. Fura to thoroughly review your medical history, perform a comprehensive examination, and discuss treatment options tailored to your specific condition.

We specialize in treating chronic pain conditions including back and neck pain, sciatica, herniated discs, arthritis, neuropathy, fibromyalgia, and pain from personal injuries or accidents. Dr. Fura uses minimally-invasive techniques to provide effective pain relief.

Prior to your procedure, you'll receive detailed instructions from our team. Generally, this includes fasting requirements, medication adjustments, and arranging transportation. We'll also review your medical history and answer any questions during your pre-procedure consultation.

Specific medication instructions vary by procedure and patient. Blood thinners may need to be stopped several days before. Never stop any medication without consulting Dr. Fura first. We'll provide personalized instructions based on your medications and procedure type.

Most patients can resume light activities within 24 hours. We typically recommend avoiding strenuous activities for 24-48 hours. Return to work depends on your job requirements and the specific procedure. Dr. Fura will provide personalized activity guidelines.

Duration of pain relief varies by procedure type and individual response. Some procedures provide temporary relief lasting weeks to months, while others like radiofrequency ablation can provide relief for 6-12 months or longer. We'll discuss expected outcomes during your consultation.
